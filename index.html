Our proposed dynamic CSN network was applied to the convolution layers over one step of Instaflow 0.9B, four steps of LCM, and fifty steps of stable diffusion v1.4. Our method can prune an average of 80G MACs per timestep without requiring retraining. In contrast, Diff-pruning and traditional methods like Taylor pruning typically achieve results similar to the unpruned model with only 5G MACs pruning.

Abstract

Stable Diffusion generates photorealistic results on a wide variety of image generation tasks such as text-to-image, inpainting, and pose-guided generation. However, the efficiency of the model and the pipeline becomes an issue for real-time demanding applications. In this work, we take a very simple yet surprisingly effective way of speeding up the stable diffusion model: skip channels in the UNet structure that are less important for the generation. We design a learnable network component that modulate the feature channels and turn off unimportant ones. Experiments show that our model can achieve similar image generation quality as the original stable diffusion model but with a fraction of the running cost.
